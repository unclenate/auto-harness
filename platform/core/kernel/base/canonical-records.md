# Canonical And Derivative Records

Canonical records are the durable source of truth. Derivative records summarize, narrate, or aid local workflow.

## Canonical

- ADRs
- PRDs (Product Requirements Documents) — numbered records capturing product decisions, their rationale, and alternatives considered; the product counterpart to ADRs
- product requirements and release intent artifacts
- problem statement and persona definitions
- project delivery logs and milestone records
- program coordination artifacts when active
- risk register
- incident records
- migration records
- release records
- discovery intake questionnaire (`docs/discovery/intake-questionnaire.md`) — living during discovery; historical context after launch
- MVP scope records (`docs/discovery/mvp-scope.md`) — the boundary that governs the build; update when scope changes, log changes
- visual starting assets log (`docs/discovery/starting-assets.md`) — provenance for requirements derived from mockups, wireframes, or prototypes; applicable when the project started from non-text artifacts

## Derivative

- session logs
- scratchpads
- dev journals
- transient planning notes
- convenience summaries produced by agents

Derivative records may be useful. They are not authoritative.
